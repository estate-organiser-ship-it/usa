Does a Will Cover Everything? What Sits Outside It

Jul 16, 2026

A will feels like the finish line. You sign it, you breathe out, done. But a will only controls part of what you leave behind. Some of the biggest and most personal things sit outside it entirely.

Retirement accounts and life insurance

Money in a 401(k), an IRA or a life insurance policy is generally paid to the beneficiary named on the account's own form, not distributed by your will. If that form is out of date, the form still usually wins. That is why checking your beneficiary designations matters as much as the will itself.

Jointly owned assets

Property held in joint names with rights of survivorship, such as many family homes and joint bank accounts, generally passes straight to the surviving owner, outside the will.

Payable-on-death accounts

Many banks let you add a payable-on-death instruction to an account. Where one is in place, the account generally goes to that named person directly.

Trusts

Anything held in a living trust is distributed by the trust's own terms, not by your will.

Your digital life

Photo libraries, email, online banking, subscriptions, loyalty points, even the phone that unlocks all of it. A will can say who gets things, but it rarely tells anyone where accounts live or how to reach them. Without a record, families lose real money and irreplaceable photos simply because nobody could find or open anything.

Your wishes

What you would want for a funeral. Who should be told, and in what order. The small personal instructions that never make it into a legal document. None of that lives in a will, and it is exactly what families agonise over in the first week.

The map that ties it together
